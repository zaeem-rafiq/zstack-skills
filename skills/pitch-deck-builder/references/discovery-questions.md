# Discovery Interview Question Bank

Use these questions as a guide during the discovery phase. Don't ask them all rigidly — adapt based on responses and ask 2-3 at a time conversationally.

## Stage 1: Foundation (3-5 questions)

Start here to understand the pitch context before diving into details.

1. **Pitch Type & Audience**: "What type of pitch deck are you creating, and who's the primary audience? For example: pre-seed investors, enterprise customers, strategic partners, internal stakeholders, etc."

2. **Core Narrative**: "In 2-3 sentences, what's the most compelling story you want to tell? Think about the transformation or opportunity you're presenting."

3. **Stage & Goals**: "What stage is your company/product at, and what's your goal for this deck? For example: raising $2M seed round, closing enterprise deals, securing partnerships, etc."

4. **Key Constraints**: "Are there any specific constraints I should know about? For example: must be under 15 slides, needs to address specific concerns, follows a particular format, etc."

5. **Timeline**: "When do you need this deck, and what's the first use case? Is there a specific meeting or deadline?"

## Stage 2: Business Fundamentals (5-8 questions)

Based on initial responses, dive into the business model and value proposition.

1. **Problem Definition**: "Describe the problem you're solving. What pain point exists today, and how acute is it for your target customers?"

2. **Solution Approach**: "What's your solution, and why is it uniquely positioned to solve this problem? What's your unfair advantage?"

3. **Market Opportunity**: "What's the market size and your addressable segment? Are there any particularly compelling market dynamics or timing factors?"

4. **Business Model**: "How do you make money? What are your unit economics or revenue model?"

5. **Traction & Validation**: "What traction or validation do you have? This could be revenue, users, partnerships, pilot results, surveys, LOIs, waitlist signups, etc."

6. **Competition**: "Who are your main competitors or alternatives, and how are you differentiated?"

7. **Why Now?**: "What makes this the right time for this solution? Is there a market shift, regulatory change, technology enabler, or cultural trend that creates urgency?"

8. **Customer Profile**: "Who is your ideal customer? Can you describe them specifically — their role, company size, pain frequency, budget authority?"

## Stage 3: Narrative Elements (4-6 questions)

Gather the human and emotional elements that make a pitch memorable.

1. **Founding Story**: "Is there a compelling founding story or insight that led to creating this company/product? A personal experience or 'aha moment'?"

2. **Customer Evidence**: "Do you have any customer quotes, case studies, or success stories that illustrate your impact? Specific numbers or transformations?"

3. **Vision**: "What's your long-term vision? Where do you see this in 3-5 years? What does the world look like if you succeed?"

4. **Team**: "What makes your team uniquely qualified? Any notable backgrounds, domain expertise, prior exits, or relevant achievements?"

5. **Milestones**: "What are your key milestones or roadmap items for the next 12-18 months? What will you accomplish with this funding/partnership?"

6. **Risk Awareness**: "What are the biggest risks or objections someone might raise, and how would you address them?"

## Stage 4: Data & Materials (3-4 questions)

Collect specific data points and existing materials for slide creation.

1. **Quantitative Data**: "What are your key metrics? For example: revenue, MRR/ARR, growth rate, user count, retention rate, CAC/LTV, margins, pipeline, conversion rates, etc."

2. **Visual Materials**: "Do you have any existing materials to incorporate? Logos, brand colors (hex codes), product screenshots, team photos, existing slides, brand guidelines?"

3. **Must-Include Elements**: "Are there any specific slides, data points, quotes, or messages that absolutely must be included?"

4. **Sensitive Topics**: "Is there anything we should be careful about or deliberately exclude? Confidential metrics, ongoing negotiations, etc.?"

## Adaptive Follow-Up Patterns

When a user gives a particularly interesting answer, dig deeper:

- **Strong traction**: "That growth is impressive. Can you break that down — what's driving it? Is it concentrated in a specific segment or channel?"
- **Unique team**: "Tell me more about [specific experience]. How does that directly translate to an advantage in building this?"
- **Big market claim**: "How did you arrive at that market size? Walk me through the TAM/SAM/SOM breakdown."
- **Vague problem**: "Can you give me a specific example of a customer experiencing this pain? What does their day look like without your solution?"
- **Competitive concern**: "What would a competitor say is their advantage over you? And how would you respond?"
