# Narrative Frameworks & Slide Sequences

## Standard Investor Pitch (12-15 slides)

Best for: Pre-seed, seed, and Series A fundraising.

1. **Cover** — Company name, tagline, contact info
2. **Problem** — The pain point, quantified if possible
3. **Solution** — Your approach and why it works
4. **Product/Demo** — How it works (screenshots, flow, demo)
5. **Market Opportunity** — TAM/SAM/SOM with compelling dynamics
6. **Business Model** — Revenue streams, pricing, unit economics
7. **Traction** — Metrics, growth trajectory, validation
8. **Competition** — Landscape and differentiation
9. **Go-to-Market** — Distribution, customer acquisition strategy
10. **Team** — Founders, key hires, advisors
11. **Financials** — Projections, key assumptions
12. **Milestones** — 12-18 month roadmap
13. **Funding Ask** — Amount, use of funds, terms
14. **Vision** — Long-term impact and ambition
15. **Contact/Thank You** — How to follow up

### Sequencing Principles for Investor Decks

- **Lead with strength**: If traction is exceptional, consider moving it to slide 3-4
- **The "why now" slide**: Often inserted after Problem or Market — explains timing/urgency
- **Team placement**: Pre-seed decks often move Team earlier (slide 4-5) since team IS the bet
- **Appendix strategy**: Put detailed financials, technical architecture, or market research in backup slides

## Product/Sales Pitch (8-12 slides)

Best for: Enterprise sales, product launches, customer acquisition.

1. **Cover** — Company name, value proposition
2. **Problem** — Customer pain point with empathy
3. **Solution Overview** — High-level approach
4. **Product Details** — Features, workflow, screenshots
5. **Benefits & ROI** — Quantified value delivery
6. **Customer Success** — Case studies, testimonials, logos
7. **Competitive Advantage** — Why you vs. alternatives
8. **Pricing/Packages** — Clear options
9. **Implementation** — Timeline, support, onboarding
10. **Company/Team** — Credibility and trust
11. **Next Steps** — Clear CTA
12. **Contact** — How to proceed

### Sequencing Principles for Sales Decks

- **Customer-centric framing**: Every slide answers "what's in it for me?"
- **Social proof early**: Move customer logos/testimonials to slide 3-4 if you have strong ones
- **ROI is king**: The benefits slide is often the most important — spend extra time on it
- **Remove friction**: Implementation and pricing should feel easy and transparent

## Partnership Pitch (8-12 slides)

Best for: Strategic partnerships, integrations, co-development.

1. **Cover** — Both company names/logos
2. **Company Overview** — Your capabilities and market position
3. **Opportunity** — Market trend or gap creating the partnership need
4. **Strategic Fit** — Why these two companies together
5. **Partnership Model** — What the collaboration looks like
6. **Mutual Benefits** — Value for both sides
7. **Case for Collaboration** — Why now, expected outcomes
8. **Proposed Next Steps** — Timeline and commitments
9. **Team/Contact** — Key people on your side

### Sequencing Principles for Partnership Decks

- **Equal framing**: Avoid making it feel one-sided — emphasize mutual value throughout
- **Specificity wins**: Vague "synergies" lose. Concrete partnership mechanics convince.
- **Quick to action**: Partnership decks should be shorter and move quickly to the proposal

## Narrative Arc Patterns

### The "Hero's Journey" Arc
Problem (ordinary world) → Solution (call to adventure) → Product (the journey) → Traction (trials & victories) → Vision (the new world)

Best for: Founder-driven stories with a personal connection to the problem.

### The "Data Escalation" Arc
Small insight → Growing evidence → Market validation → Explosive growth data → Inevitable conclusion

Best for: Data-rich companies with strong metrics that build a compelling case.

### The "Inevitable Future" Arc
Market shift → Why now → Our unique position → Early proof → The ask to seize the moment

Best for: Companies riding a macro trend (AI, regulation change, demographic shift).

### The "Before/After" Arc
World today (painful) → World tomorrow (transformed) → How we bridge the gap → Proof it works → Join us

Best for: Companies with dramatic customer transformation stories.

## Slide Placement Strategy

### The "Power Zone" (Slides 6-9 in a 15-slide deck)

This is where your strongest material goes. By this point you've set context and built interest. The audience is most attentive and hasn't yet started thinking about objections. Place your best traction data, most impressive growth charts, or strongest competitive moats here.

### Opening Hook (Slides 1-3)

You have roughly 60-90 seconds to earn attention. The opening must:
- Establish credibility immediately
- Present a problem the audience recognizes and cares about
- Hint at a compelling solution

### Closing Momentum (Slides 12-15)

End on forward motion:
- Milestones create a sense of inevitability
- The ask should feel like a natural conclusion, not a surprise
- Vision should be ambitious but believable
- Leave them wanting to continue the conversation
